Mulch Services

  • Basic Mulch Installation: Basic mulch installation typically starts at around $1 to $3 per square foot. This price includes the cost of mulch material and labor for spreading it.
  • Mulch Material Cost: The type of mulch you choose can significantly impact the price. Organic mulches like wood chips or bark mulch tend to be less expensive, usually ranging from $30 to $50 per cubic yard. Decorative mulches like colored or stone mulch can be pricier, ranging from $40 to $100 or more per cubic yard.
  • Delivery Fees: If you order mulch in bulk, you may incur delivery fees, which can vary based on the distance and quantity ordered. Delivery fees often range from $50 to $150 or more.
  • Mulch Depth: The depth at which you apply mulch can also affect the cost. A standard depth of 2-3 inches is common, but deeper mulching may require more material and, therefore, cost more.
  • Weed Barrier Fabric: If you opt for weed barrier fabric to be installed beneath the mulch, this can add an additional $0.20 to $0.50 per square foot to the overall cost.
  • Additional Services: If your landscaping company offers services like weed removal, bed edging, or mulch refreshment, these may come with additional charges.

Tips for maintaining your mulch

  • Regularly Fluff and Turn: Mulch can become compacted over time. Use a rake or pitchfork to fluff and turn the mulch periodically, especially after heavy rain or when it settles. This will help maintain its appearance and aerate the soil underneath.
  • Top Off as Needed: Mulch gradually decomposes, so it's necessary to top it off annually or as it thins out. Adding a fresh layer of mulch not only improves the appearance but also enhances its effectiveness in retaining moisture and suppressing weeds.
  • Weed Control: Mulch is an excellent weed suppressant, but weeds may occasionally break through. Regularly inspect your mulch beds for weeds and remove them promptly to prevent them from taking root.
  • Edge Your Mulch Beds: Define the boundaries of your mulch beds with edging material like bricks, stones, or plastic edging. This creates clean, well-defined lines and helps prevent mulch from spilling onto the surrounding areas.
  • Proper Mulch Depth: Maintain the recommended mulch depth of 2-3 inches. Applying too much mulch can lead to excess moisture retention and may suffocate plant roots. Conversely, too little mulch won't provide adequate weed control or moisture retention.
  • Monitor Moisture Levels: Ensure that your mulch beds receive sufficient moisture, especially during dry spells. Adequate moisture helps prevent the mulch from drying out and becoming brittle.
  • Mulch Types: Consider the type of mulch you're using. Some mulch varieties, like shredded bark or wood chips, may break down faster than others. Choose mulch based on your aesthetic preferences and maintenance schedule.
  • Remove Old Mulch: If your mulch has decomposed significantly or developed mold or fungal issues, it's a good idea to remove the old mulch and replace it with fresh material.
  • Weed Barrier Fabric: Consider using weed barrier fabric beneath your mulch to further deter weed growth. This can reduce the maintenance required to keep your mulch beds looking tidy.
  • Professional Maintenance: If maintaining your mulch beds becomes too challenging or time-consuming, consider hiring a professional landscaper to handle mulch installation and maintenance. They have the expertise to keep your mulch looking its best year-round.
